What Digital Marketing Agencies Look For When Evaluating Website Performance in 2025

Directly to the point. So, evaluating websites for 2025, it’s… not just about traffic anymore, you know? Honestly, it’s shifted. Big time. ## What we check, really Okay, so what agencies are actually looking at. It’s a lot, but also, it boils down to a few key things. * **User Experience (UX)**: This is huge. Always was, but now… it's everything. * **Core Web Vitals**: Yeah, still critical. But, like, INP, that Interaction to Next Paint thing? That’s really, really a big deal now. Responsiveness. People expect instant. * **Mobile-first, for real**: Not just responsive, but actually *designed* for mobile. The whole flow. * **AI Search Readiness**: How does the site perform with AI-driven search, or even those AI overviews? * **Structured Data**: Gotta have it. Clearly marked, easy for AI to parse. * **Content Authority**: E-E-A-T, still floating around. Proving you know your stuff. That's kinda foundational, right? * **Data Strategy (First-Party)**: This is crucial, honestly. With cookies going away… * **Consent Management**: How’s that handled? Is it clear? Compliant? * **CRM Integration**: Can they actually use their own data effectively? Personalization? * **Personalization & Adaptability**: Sites need to feel… individual. * **Dynamic Content**: Can it show different stuff to different users based on their history? * **Journey Mapping**: Are they thinking about the *whole* customer journey, not just landing pages? * **Accessibility**: I mean, this is non-negotiable. Is it inclusive? For everyone? Simple as that. ## Stuff people miss A lot of sites… they just miss the deeper stuff. They optimize for yesterday. Like, not really thinking about *how* people are interacting with information now, or how AI is changing that. Or, honestly, they just slap on some structured data and call it a day. But it’s more than that. It’s about the *intent* behind the content, the user's *actual* needs. Yeah. That's it.
